Early Leadership: The "Man of the House"

My leadership journey began at a young age when my father moved abroad for work, leaving me as the "man of the house." With the responsibility of supporting my mother and two younger sisters, I learned to lead, prioritize, and manage — skills that would later become essential in my professional life.

Building a Foundation in Mechanical Engineering

Academically, I thrived and pursued a degree in Mechanical Engineering at the National School of Monastir. During my time there, I began sharpening my project management skills, particularly through organizing events. One of my key experiences was leading the communication efforts for the first edition of the National Design Competition. This hands-on role helped me understand the nuances of managing people, tasks, and timelines.

Internships: Gaining Technical Experience

In my first two years, I completed five internships across five different cities. While these roles were highly technical and often lacked the team-based interactions I hoped for, they helped me lay a solid foundation in practical engineering. These experiences, although individual in nature, were essential in preparing me for larger team-based projects later in my career.

My First Real Leadership Experience: The DMAIC Project

In February 2018, I finally had the opportunity to apply my project management knowledge in the real world. Over five months, I led a DMAIC project, working closely with a multidisciplinary team. This was my first experience organizing a kick-off meeting, managing quality and communication, and overseeing both the team and the project’s budget. It was a pivotal moment that strengthened my leadership skills.

Empowering the Team and Achieving Success

By the end of the project, we met the KPIs set by our sponsor, a success I’m particularly proud of. However, the most rewarding aspect was empowering my team. I conducted several training sessions, helping my teammates build new skills and develop professionally. Watching them grow throughout the project was an invaluable experience for me as a leader.

Moving Forward: New Opportunities Ahead

This success opened doors for me. Even before I officially presented the project to my university to receive my diploma, I was invited for a job interview. I still remember taking a day off from work to attend my diploma ceremony, knowing that this project had already started shaping my career and future in project management.
