My Journey to Earning the Green Belt Certification

Embarking on the journey to obtain my Green Belt certification has been a transformative experience, filled with challenges, learning opportunities, and personal growth. In this blog, I want to share my pathway, the pivotal moments, and the valuable lessons I learned along the way.

Starting with a Strong Foundation

My journey began during my final project at university, where I had the opportunity to lead a SMED (Single-Minute Exchange of Die) project. This experience was made possible by several internal training sessions that equipped me with the essential tools and knowledge. Leading this project ignited my passion for continuous improvement and laid the groundwork for my future endeavors in Lean methodologies.

Diving into the Black Belt Project

At the beginning of my professional career, I participated in the first Black Belt project at Safran in Tunisia. This experience was eye-opening; I learned from experts in the field and was able to witness firsthand the impact of effective problem-solving techniques. Contributing to this project solidified my interest in Lean Six Sigma and motivated me to further my education in this area.

Online Learning: Yellow Belt and Green Belt

To deepen my understanding, I enrolled in online courses on Coursera, successfully completing both the Yellow Belt and Green Belt certifications. These courses provided me with theoretical knowledge and practical insights, allowing me to grasp the concepts of DMAIC (Define, Measure, Analyze, Improve, Control) thoroughly.

Contributing to a Green Belt Project

I also had the opportunity to participate as a team member in a Green Belt project. This hands-on experience allowed me to apply what I had learned and collaborate with others passionate about process improvement. It was an invaluable experience that reinforced my commitment to this field.

Leading a Green Belt Project During COVID-19

During the COVID-19 pandemic, I was fortunate to lead a Green Belt project. Before taking on this role, I completed six days of in-person training focused on developing leadership skills and change management strategies. Additionally, I finished a 20-hour MOOC with a score exceeding 90%. This preparation was crucial as it equipped me with the skills necessary to lead a project in challenging times.

The DMAIC Process and Validation

Throughout each phase of the DMAIC process, I presented milestones to the steering committee, which included my project sponsor and coach. Their feedback was instrumental in refining our approach and ensuring we stayed aligned with project goals. After the project was internally validated by the finance department regarding the realized gains, I delivered a final presentation online to the seats branch committee for ultimate approval.

Conclusion

Earning my Green Belt certification has been a rewarding journey filled with learning and growth. I hope my experiences inspire others to pursue their own paths in Lean Six Sigma. As I continue on this journey, I look forward to applying my knowledge to drive positive change in my organization and beyond.
