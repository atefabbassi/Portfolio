PMP Certification Process

The Project Management Institute (PMI)® offers the Project Management Professional (PMP)® certification, a highly esteemed credential recognized globally. According to PMI®, PMP certification signifies the application of knowledge, skills, and methodologies essential for successful project completion. This guide will lead you through each stage of the PMP® certification process, from determining your eligibility to renewing your Professional Development Units (PDUs).

PMP® Certification Eligibility:

To qualify for the PMP® Certification, you must have substantial project management experience and training. Ensure you meet the following eligibility criteria before applying:

A four-year degree

36 months of leading projects

35 hours of project management education/training or CAPM® Certification

Or :

A high school diploma or an associate degree (or global equivalent)

60 months of leading projects

35 hours of project management education/training or CAPM® Certification

It's crucial to accurately document all these requirements; ensure that test results, certificates, and transcripts are available to support your claims.

Completing the PMP® Examination Application:

Once you meet the eligibility requirements, the next step is to fill out the examination application form. You can access the online application by creating an account on the PMI® website and navigating to the "my PMI" section. For offline submission, you may download the paper form and mail it to the PMI® office in the US via registered mail.

When using the online application system, complete the form within 90 days. Your data will be stored on PMI® servers for this period, after which it will be deleted. Key sections of the form include:

Personal details (name, address, contact information, etc.)

Education background (degree information)

Total contact hours

A detailed list of projects demonstrating your project management experience

You should receive a confirmation of acceptance within a week of submitting the application.

PMP® Membership & Fees:

After submitting your application, PMI® will email you instructions to pay the exam fees. The current fee is $405 for members and $555 for non-members. While PMI® membership is optional, joining offers numerous benefits, including a free digital copy of the PMBOK® Guide and access to e-books, on-demand webinars, and peer-reviewed articles.

PMI® Audit Process:

Once the exam fees are paid, you will be immediately informed if your application is selected for an audit. If chosen, you have three months to provide verification for each item listed in your application, such as:

Proof of education (a photocopy of your diploma)

Evidence of contact hours (certificates of completion)

Reference letters signed by the contact persons listed on your projects

These documents must be sent to PMI® in separate, officially sealed envelopes by registered mail. If the primary contact person is unavailable, another individual knowledgeable about your role may complete the process. You should receive a confirmation email within a week of submitting your documents.

PMP® Examination Fee Refund Policy:

If you decide not to take the PMP® exam, you must submit a withdrawal request to PMI® at least 30 days before your exam eligibility expires. If you haven’t scheduled or taken the exam, you will receive a refund minus a $100 processing fee. If you fail the audit, you will receive the same amount.

Scheduling the PMP® Certification Exam:

After passing the audit and meeting other prerequisites, you can schedule your exam. Log in to the PMI Certification system and choose between the Paper-Based Test (PBT) or Computer-Based Test (CBT). The exam must be taken within one year of paying the exam fee.

Exam Preparation and Familiarization with PMP® Question Patterns:

The PMP® certification journey is highly individualized. In addition to the Exam Content Outline (ECO), PMBOK® Guide, and study tools, you should develop a solid study plan, use authorized materials, take realistic mock exams, and track your learning progress.

The PMaspire PMP® Exam Simulator is a web-based platform designed to help you prepare for the PMP® exam. It provides a simulated environment accessible from any device, allowing you to practice and build confidence for the final test. While PMI® does not specify a passing score, it is commonly believed that a score of 61% is required to pass. To increase your chances of success, aim to score between 65% and 80% on each simulated exam.
