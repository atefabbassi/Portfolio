My Journey into Project Management: From Engineering to PMO

Foundations in Mechanical Engineering

My path to project management began with a strong technical foundation. After earning my diploma in mechanical engineering in 2018, I started my career at Safran Cabine. Just one month into the role, I was given the opportunity to participate in a Black Belt Lean Six Sigma project—the first of its kind at Safran Tunisie. The success of this project laid the groundwork for my future as a project manager, as it earned me the trust of my managers and opened the door to more complex projects.

Certifications and Early Project Management

In 2019, I became certified in Prévention des Risques liés à l’Activité Physique (PRAP) through INRS France, which equipped me to lead ergonomic projects at Safran Cabine Tunisie. As the first PRAP referent in the company, I spearheaded various initiatives focused on safety and workplace efficiency. Although the certification was valid for only two years, it significantly influenced my approach to risk management and team leadership.

Innovative Projects and Leadership Growth

My next significant career milestone came when I joined the 'Dynamic Line' project for galley assembly, which later became a finalist in the Safran Innovation Awards. After the initial project manager was promoted, I stepped in as their replacement, gaining invaluable experience in project leadership. I also had the opportunity to collaborate with teams from Safran Cabin Czech, where we exchanged ideas and replicated successful projects, further expanding my exposure to international project management.

Managing Projects in Challenging Times

In 2020, amid the global COVID-19 crisis, I faced one of my biggest career challenges. After an interview with HR, I was appointed project manager for industrialization at Safran Composite, overseeing the development of advanced composite products for helicopters. Just one week after joining the team, I was leading my first customer meeting. This period helped me refine my skills in adapting to high-stakes environments while balancing innovation with rapid execution.
